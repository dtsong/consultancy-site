## The Challenge

A Fortune 500 manufacturing company had been running their enterprise data warehouse on on-premises SQL Server for over a decade. The platform processed data from 12 manufacturing facilities, 200+ suppliers, and multiple ERP instances. But the system was reaching its limits.

Nightly ETL jobs that once completed in 4 hours were now taking 14+ hours, regularly bleeding into business hours. The monolithic SSIS package architecture made changes risky and slow — a simple business logic modification required a 6-week development and testing cycle. And the hardware refresh cycle was approaching, with a projected $3.2M capital expenditure.

The CTO's mandate was clear: migrate to the cloud, but without disrupting the 2,000+ business users who depended on daily reports and dashboards.

## Our Approach

### Phase 1: Assessment and Architecture (Weeks 1-4)

We conducted a thorough assessment of the existing platform — cataloging 340+ SSIS packages, 1,200+ stored procedures, and 85 downstream reporting connections. We mapped data lineage, identified transformation logic, and assessed data quality baseline metrics.

Our architecture recommendation: **Azure Synapse Analytics** as the core warehouse with **Azure Data Factory** for orchestration and **dbt** for transformation logic. This combination provided the scalability needed while enabling modern development practices like version-controlled transformations and automated testing.

### Phase 2: Foundation and Parallel Run (Weeks 5-16)

We built the Azure landing zone with proper networking, security, and governance. Then we began migrating workloads in priority order, starting with the supply chain analytics domain — the area with the highest business impact and most urgent performance problems.

Each migrated workload ran in parallel with the legacy system for a minimum of two weeks, with automated reconciliation checks comparing outputs row-by-row. This approach caught 23 subtle logic discrepancies in the legacy system that had gone undetected for years.

### Phase 3: Cutover and Optimization (Weeks 17-24)

With parallel runs validated, we executed domain-by-domain cutovers during planned maintenance windows. Post-migration, we implemented performance optimizations that took advantage of cloud-native capabilities — materialized views, result set caching, and workload isolation for different user groups.

## The Results

The migrated platform processes the same data volumes in under 3 hours — an **80% reduction** in processing time. But the real transformation was in agility: the development cycle for business logic changes dropped from 6 weeks to 5 days, enabled by dbt's modular approach and automated testing.

Infrastructure costs decreased by 40% compared to the projected hardware refresh, with additional savings from eliminating database administration overhead. The total annual savings exceeded **$2M**.

Most importantly, the platform now supports real-time data ingestion for critical manufacturing metrics, enabling the operations team to respond to quality issues in minutes rather than the next business day.
